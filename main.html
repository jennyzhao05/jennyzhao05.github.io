Data Science & Biology Major, Northeastern University

I am a Data Science and Biology major at Northeastern University with a deep interest in the intersection of computational methods and the life sciences. My academic journey has strengthened my passion for applying data-driven approaches—such as statistical modeling, machine learning, and data visualization—to uncover meaningful insights in biological and medical research. I am particularly fascinated by how computational analysis can help decode complex biological systems and accelerate the development of new therapies.


I am currently seeking opportunities to expand my expertise in biostatistics, computational biology, biological wet-lab research, and translational data analysis. Through these experiences, I aim to deepen my understanding of how quantitative tools can bridge the gap between biological discovery and real-world healthcare applications. Outside the classroom, I enjoy working on independent projects that combine programming, data analysis, and scientific inquiry, driven by curiosity and a genuine desire to solve impactful problems.


I am always eager to take on new challenges that sharpen my technical and analytical skills, broaden my interdisciplinary perspective, and contribute to projects at the intersection of data science and biology that have the potential to make meaningful contributions to science and society.

Highlighted Project

My project, Statewide University Tuition & Rating Analysis, investigates whether higher tuition costs at U.S. private universities correlate with better educational quality. By integrating data scraping, statistical modeling, and visualization, it provides insights to help students make informed educational decisions.
